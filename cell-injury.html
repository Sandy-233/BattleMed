📖 Notes

Definition

Cell injury is the functional and structural change occurring when cells cannot adapt to stress.

Causes

  • Hypoxia
  • Ischemia
  • Chemical injury
  • Physical agents
  • Infections
  • Immune reactions
  • Nutritional disorders
  • Genetic defects

Mechanism

⭐ High Yield for NEET PG

  • ATP depletion is the earliest biochemical change.
  • Mitochondria are the first organelles affected.
  • Calcium influx activates destructive enzymes.
  • Reversible injury can recover if the cause is removed.
  • Irreversible injury results in necrosis or apoptosis.

📖 Chapter Overview

Cell injury is one of the highest-yield topics in pathology and forms the basis for understanding inflammation, necrosis, apoptosis and systemic pathology. This chapter explains why cells get injured, how they respond and when injury becomes irreversible.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand causes of cell injury
  • Differentiate reversible vs irreversible injury
  • Understand ATP depletion
  • Understand calcium influx
  • Differentiate necrosis and apoptosis
  • Know Robbins high-yield points
  • Solve clinical questions confidently
