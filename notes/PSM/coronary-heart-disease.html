❤️ Coronary Heart Disease (CHD)

Definition

Coronary Heart Disease is a condition in which blood supply to the heart muscle becomes reduced because of narrowing or blockage of the coronary arteries. The most common cause is atherosclerosis.

Exam Definition Coronary Heart Disease is myocardial damage resulting from reduced coronary blood flow due to narrowing or obstruction of coronary arteries.

Epidemiology

Feature Description
Major Cause Atherosclerosis

Clinical Features

Category Features
Chest Symptoms Central chest discomfort, pressure or heaviness, discomfort during exertion, relief with rest in stable disease.
Associated Symptoms Breathlessness, sweating, nausea, fatigue, palpitations.
Advanced Disease Reduced exercise tolerance, features of heart failure, dizziness or syncope in severe cases.
Clinical Pearl Patients may present with typical chest discomfort or with less specific symptoms such as breathlessness or unusual fatigue, especially older adults.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is based on clinical assessment together with appropriate investigations. Risk-factor evaluation and assessment of symptoms help determine the likelihood of coronary heart disease.

History ↓ Physical Examination ↓ ECG ↓ Cardiac Biomarkers (when indicated) ↓ Cardiac Imaging / Functional Tests ↓ Diagnosis and Risk Assessment

Physical Examination

  • Measure blood pressure.
  • Record pulse rate and rhythm.
  • Assess body mass index and waist circumference.
  • Look for signs of heart failure.
  • Assess cardiovascular risk factors.

Prevention

Healthy Lifestyle ↓ Control Risk Factors ↓ Early Detection ↓ Appropriate Treatment ↓ Reduced CHD Events

Prevention of Coronary Heart Disease focuses on reducing modifiable risk factors through healthy lifestyle practices, regular health check-ups, and appropriate medical care for high-risk individuals.

Primary Prevention

  • Avoid tobacco in all forms.
  • Maintain healthy body weight.
  • Regular physical activity.
  • Balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Reduce excess salt, sugar and saturated fat intake.
  • Control blood pressure.
  • Control blood glucose.
  • Manage blood lipid levels.
  • Stress management.
  • Regular health screening.

Secondary Prevention

Secondary prevention aims to reduce future cardiovascular events in individuals already diagnosed with Coronary Heart Disease.

  • Regular follow-up.
  • Strict control of blood pressure.
  • Control diabetes.
  • Lipid management.
  • Smoking cessation.
  • Cardiac rehabilitation.
  • Medication adherence.

Tertiary Prevention

  • Prevent disability.
  • Improve quality of life.
  • Cardiac rehabilitation programmes.
  • Exercise training.
  • Dietary counselling.
  • Psychological support.
  • Long-term follow-up.

National Programme for Prevention and Control of Non-Communicable Diseases (NPCDCS)

NPCDCS is a national health programme of the Government of India launched to prevent and control major non-communicable diseases through health promotion, early diagnosis, treatment, referral services and regular follow-up.

Objectives

  • Reduce premature deaths due to NCDs.
  • Promote healthy lifestyle in the community.
  • Early detection of hypertension, diabetes and common cancers.
  • Provide affordable treatment at all healthcare levels.
  • Prevent complications through continuous follow-up.
  • Strengthen health infrastructure for NCD management.

Diseases Covered

Investigation Purpose
Electrocardiogram (ECG) Evaluates heart rhythm and evidence of ischemic changes.
Cardiac Biomarkers Support assessment when myocardial injury is suspected.
Echocardiography Assesses cardiac structure and ventricular function.
Disease Group Examples
Cardiovascular Diseases Hypertension, Coronary Heart Disease, Stroke
Diabetes Type 1 & Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
Cancers Oral, Breast and Cervical Cancer
Chronic Respiratory Diseases Asthma and COPD

Target Population

  • Adults aged 30 years and above.
  • Individuals with risk factors.
  • People with obesity, smoking or alcohol use.
  • People with family history of NCDs.

Population-Based Screening

Adults ≥30 Years ↓ Community Screening ↓ Blood Pressure ↓ Blood Sugar ↓ Oral, Breast & Cervical Cancer Screening ↓ Referral ↓ Treatment ↓ Regular Follow-up

Services at Different Levels

Health Facility Services
Health & Wellness Centre Health education, screening and referral.
Primary Health Centre Diagnosis, treatment and follow-up.
Community Health Centre Specialist consultation and investigations.
District Hospital NCD Clinic and comprehensive management.
Medical College Advanced treatment and referral services.

Health Promotion

  • Tobacco cessation.
  • Alcohol reduction.
  • Healthy balanced diet.
  • Regular physical activity.
  • Weight management.
  • Stress reduction.
  • Periodic health check-ups.

Referral System

Community ↓ Health & Wellness Centre ↓ PHC ↓ CHC ↓ District Hospital ↓ Medical College
Mnemonic: SCREEN
  • S → Screening
  • C → Control Risk Factors
  • R → Referral
  • E → Early Detection
  • E → Education
  • N → NCD Management
NEET PG High Yield
  • Population-based screening starts from 30 years.
  • Focus diseases: CVDs, Diabetes, Cancer and Chronic Respiratory Diseases.
  • Main strategy: Prevention, Early Detection, Treatment and Follow-up.
  • Lifestyle modification is the cornerstone of the programme.

National Targets under NPCDCS

Target Goal
Tobacco Use Reduce prevalence through awareness and cessation services.
Physical Inactivity Promote regular exercise and active lifestyles.
Hypertension Increase early detection and improve blood pressure control.
Diabetes Early diagnosis and effective long-term management.
Cancer Screening, early diagnosis and timely treatment.

Role of Health Workers

  • Identify individuals at high risk.
  • Conduct population-based screening.
  • Measure blood pressure and blood glucose.
  • Provide counselling on diet and exercise.
  • Educate regarding tobacco and alcohol cessation.
  • Ensure treatment compliance.
  • Maintain records and follow-up.
  • Refer patients needing higher care.

Summary Flowchart

Health Promotion ↓ Population Screening ↓ Early Diagnosis ↓ Treatment ↓ Follow-up ↓ Complication Prevention ↓ Healthy Community
